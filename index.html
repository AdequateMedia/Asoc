About True Worshippers

Founded in 2020 by Apostle Solomon S. Adebowale and Reverend Mrs. Debby Adebowale, True Worshippers Evangelical Ministries International is a spiritual family dedicated to raising authentic worshipers who encounter God in spirit and truth.

We are called to equip believers for the work of ministry, build up the body of Christ, and carry the message of reconciliation to the ends of the earth. Through sound teaching, daily devotionals, and worship, we foster an atmosphere where lives are transformed and identity in Christ is established.

Vision & Missions

Our divine mandate and core callings as a ministry

Vision

To raise a global family of authentic worshipers whose lives reflect the nature of the Father and who impact their world with the Gospel. We envision believers walking in spiritual maturity, cultural influence, and kingdom purpose.

"That they may be called oaks of righteousness, the planting of the Lord, that he may be glorified." — Isaiah 61:3

Worship

Igniting authentic worship through praise, prayer, and the Word, drawing believers into deeper intimacy with God and creating an atmosphere for His presence. We equip worship leaders and intercessors to lead with passion and truth.

"God is spirit, and those who worship him must worship in spirit and truth." — John 4:24

Discipleship

Establishing every believer in their identity in Christ through systematic teaching, life-transforming relationships, and the daily devotional "Sequences of God." We build foundations for spiritual growth and leadership development.

"Go therefore and make disciples of all nations... teaching them to observe all that I have commanded you." — Matthew 28:19-20

Outreach & Missions

Demonstrating God's love through compassion projects, evangelism, church planting, and equipping the next generation of leaders locally and internationally. We extend the kingdom through mercy and justice.

"But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit has come upon you, and you will be my witnesses... to the end of the earth." — Acts 1:8

WHEN SEEMS NO STRENGTH

Apostle Segun D. A. & Reverend Mrs. Debby F. A. Solomon
Hymn: “He giveth more grace”
"Weakness is not a sign of God's absence; it is the platform for His power to land." – Apostle Segun D. A. Solomon

2 Corinthians 12:9-10 & Isaiah 40:29-31

“My grace is sufficient for you, for My strength is made perfect in weakness.” Therefore most gladly I will rather boast in my infirmities, that the power of Christ may rest upon me. … For when I am weak, then I am strong.

He gives power to the weak, and to those who have no might He increases strength. Even the youths shall faint and be weary, and the young men shall utterly fall, but those who wait on the Lord shall renew their strength.

Devotional Message

There are seasons when strength evaporates like morning mist. You wake up and the weight of the day feels unbearable. Prayers seem empty, the Word feels dry, and your spirit whispers, “I can't go on.” It is in that precise moment that heaven leans close.

Apostle Segun D. A. Solomon teaches: “God does not panic when your strength runs out—He prepared for that moment. His grace is not an additive to your ability; it is the entire replacement when your ability is gone.” The apostle Paul understood this secret: he stopped pretending to be strong and started boasting in his weaknesses, because that is where Christ's power rested most fully.

Reverend Mrs. Debby F. A. Solomon adds: “Many believers fight to hide their exhaustion, but God is asking you to hand Him the empty vessel so He can fill it. You were never designed to carry the load alone. The yoke is easy and the burden is light—if you let Him bear it with you.”

Today, if you feel you have no strength left, you are in the perfect place for a miracle. Stop striving. Stop pretending. Turn your emptiness toward heaven and let the Creator of the stars breathe strength into your dust. Those who wait on the Lord—not those who rush—shall renew their strength. They shall mount up with wings like eagles. They shall run and not be weary; they shall walk and not faint.

Prophetic Prayer Points

Father, I admit my weakness and I ask for Your strength to be made perfect in me today.
Lord, remove every mask of pretense and help me to rely wholly on Your grace.
I receive fresh oil, fresh fire, and supernatural strength from the Holy Spirit.
I bind every spirit of burnout, weariness, and discouragement sent to derail my destiny.
Father, teach me to wait on You and not run ahead of Your timing.
I declare that I am strengthened with all might according to Your glorious power.
Lord, let Your power rest upon me even in my most fragile moments.
I receive the grace to persevere and not lose heart, for my help comes from the Lord.
Father, renew my youth like the eagle’s; let me soar above every challenge.
I thank You that when I am weak, then I am strong—because Christ lives in me.

Word of Encouragement

Precious one, listen: the very fact that you feel your strength draining is proof that you have been carrying weight that was never yours to carry. Lay it down at the feet of Jesus. He does not despise your weakness—He wants to exchange it for His strength. You may feel like you are walking on empty, but heaven’s reserve is infinite. Take a deep breath and whisper, “Jesus, I can't, but You can.” Then watch how He shows up. The path ahead is not walked in your power, but in His. Keep going, warrior—your help has arrived.

“My grace is sufficient for you.” That is your portion today.

— Apostle D.S.A. Solomon
